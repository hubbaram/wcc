Rosalind Franklin

You look at science (or at least talk of it) as some sort of demoralising invention of man, something apart from real life, and which must be cautiously guarded and kept separate from everyday existence. But science and everyday life cannot and should not be separated.

Rosalind Franklin is known for her contributions to the understanding of DNA, RNA, coal, graphite, and viruses. She was a chemist and an x-ray crystallographer. Her work with x-ray diffraction studies contributed to the double helix theory of DNA. She was not recognized for her contributions to this discovery until after her death.

Interesting Facts

  1. She was born in Notting Hill, London.
  2. She was a gifted student with special talent for science, Latin, and sports.
  3. She pioneered work on the structure of the polio virus.
  4. She loved to travel.
